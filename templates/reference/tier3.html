Tier 3

Consonant Variants

Korean has three versions of some consonants: plain, aspirated (with air), and tense (harder).

The Pattern

  • Plain: Basic consonant (soft, between voiced and unvoiced)
  • Aspirated: Extra line = extra air (like a puff of breath)
  • Tense/Doubled: Written twice = tenser, no air released
Did you know? The strokes are a sound intensity dial

King Sejong's team designed a visual system where adding strokes = adding intensity:

ㄱ → ㅋ (+1 stroke = aspirated)
ㄱ → ㄲ (doubled = tense)

The same logic applies to all consonant families. Once you see the pattern, you can predict what a new character sounds like just by counting its strokes.

Source: Origin of Hangul - Wikipedia

Aspirated Consonants (4)

These have a strong puff of air. Hold your hand in front of your mouth - you should feel the breath.

Tense Consonants (5)

These are tenser and sharper, with no air released. Think of the sound in "uh-oh" or stopping a sound abruptly.

kk
like 'ck' in sticky
ㄸ
tt
like 'tt' in butter
ㅃ

like 'pp' in happy
ㅆ
ss
like 'ss' in hiss
ㅉ
jj
like 'dg' in edge

Don't Stress About Pronunciation

Even native Koreans sometimes can't explain the difference between these sounds. Focus on recognition first - you'll develop the pronunciation through exposure.

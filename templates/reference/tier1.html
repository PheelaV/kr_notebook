Did you know? Consonants are pictures of your mouth

The five basic consonant shapes represent what your mouth does when making the sound:

  • ㄱ — tongue touching the back of the mouth (velar)
  • ㄴ — tongue touching the upper gums (alveolar)
  • ㅁ — the shape of closed lips (bilabial)
  • ㅅ — the shape of teeth (dental)
  • ㅇ — the shape of the open throat (glottal)

King Sejong's scholars literally drew simplified diagrams of the speech organs. Hangul is the only alphabet designed with phonetics in mind from day one.

Source: Origin of Hangul - Wikipedia

Did you know? Vowels represent heaven, earth, and humanity

All vowels are built from just three elements based on Eastern philosophy:

  • • (dot) — the round sun/heaven (yang)
  • ㅡ (horizontal) — the flat earth (yin)
  • ㅣ (vertical) — a person standing upright (neutral)

This is the Samjae (Three Powers) — the idea that humans stand between heaven and earth.

Vowels with the dot/line pointing up or right (ㅗ ㅏ) are "bright" (yang). Vowels with it pointing down or left (ㅜ ㅓ) are "dark" (yin).

Source: FutureLearn - Three Ingredients of Hangeul

Basic Vowels (6)

Letter Sound Type Hint
ㅣ i neutral Like 'ee' in see
ㅓ eo yin Like 'u' in fun or 'uh'
ㅏ a yang Like 'a' in father
ㅡ eu neutral Like 'u' in put, unrounded lips
ㅜ u yin Like 'oo' in moon
ㅗ o yang Like 'o' in go

Memory Aid

  • Vertical vowels (ㅣ ㅓ ㅏ): consonant goes LEFT of the vowel
  • Horizontal vowels (ㅡ ㅜ ㅗ): consonant goes ABOVE the vowel
  • Yin vowels (ㅓ ㅜ): stroke points left/down → darker sounds
  • Yang vowels (ㅏ ㅗ): stroke points right/up → brighter sounds

How to Use Hangul Learn

Quick Start

  1. Go to Study to start learning
  2. Look at the Korean letter on the card
  3. Try to recall its sound
  4. Tap the card to reveal the answer
  5. Rate how well you remembered it
  6. Repeat daily for best results

How Spaced Repetition Works

This app uses the SM-2 algorithm, the same system behind popular apps like Anki. The idea is simple: review cards just before you forget them.

New cards appear frequently at first. As you learn them, the intervals between reviews grow longer:

1 day → 6 days → 15 days → 1 month → 3 months...

If you forget a card, it resets to a shorter interval so you see it more often until you learn it properly.

Rating Your Answers

After revealing the answer, rate how well you remembered it:

Again
Didn't remember at all. Card resets and will appear again soon.
Hard
Struggled but got it. Card resets for more practice.
Good
Remembered with some effort. Interval increases normally.
Easy
Knew it instantly. Interval increases more, card becomes easier.

Tip: Be honest with your ratings. It's better to see a card more often than to mark it "Easy" when you're not sure.

Learning Tiers

Hangul is organized into 4 tiers, from basic to advanced. You start with Tier 1 and unlock new tiers as you progress.

Tier 1: Basic Consonants & Vowels
The 9 basic consonants (ㄱ, ㄴ, ㄷ...) and 6 basic vowels (ㅏ, ㅓ, ㅗ...)
Tier 2: Y-Vowels & Special Ieung
Y-vowels (ㅑ, ㅕ, ㅛ, ㅠ), ae/e vowels (ㅐ, ㅔ), and ㅇ rules
Tier 3: Aspirated & Tense Consonants
Aspirated (ㅋ, ㅍ, ㅌ, ㅊ) and double/tense consonants (ㄲ, ㅃ, ㄸ, ㅆ, ㅉ)
Tier 4: Compound Vowels
Combined vowels like ㅘ, ㅝ, ㅟ, ㅚ, ㅢ, and more

How to unlock: Learn 80% of the cards in your current tier, then visit the Progress page to unlock the next tier.

Tips for Success

  • ✓ Study daily. Even 5 minutes a day is better than long irregular sessions.
  • ✓ Review all due cards. The system works best when you don't skip reviews.
  • ✓ Say the sounds aloud. This reinforces memory better than silent reading.
  • ✓ Master basics first. Don't rush to unlock new tiers before you're ready.
  • ✓ Be patient. It typically takes 2-3 weeks of daily practice to learn all of Hangul.

What "Learned" Means

A card is considered "learned" when you've answered it correctly at least twice in a row. This means:

  • First correct answer: interval becomes 1 day
  • Second correct answer: interval becomes 6 days
  • After that, intervals keep growing

Cards will still appear for review even after they're "learned" - that's how spaced repetition keeps them in your long-term memory.

Practice Mode

When you've reviewed all due cards, you can keep practicing with Practice Mode.

  • Shows random cards from your unlocked tiers
  • Doesn't affect your spaced repetition schedule
  • Great for extra drilling when you want more practice

Access practice mode from the home page when no cards are due, or after completing your daily reviews.
